Bunge — Technical Requirements Summary

Source: Pre-call alignment meeting  |  13 July 2026  |  Participants: Tiago (Bunge Infrastructure), Kim, Dom, Doug, Shane (IBM)

1. Background & Context

This meeting was a preparatory alignment call between the IBM team and Tiago (Bunge) ahead of a broader Wednesday session including Curtis, Eduardo, Richard Broca, and potentially Rob. The purpose was to compare notes on where Bunge's observability, asset management, and consolidation efforts stand before presenting IBM's recommendations to a wider Bunge audience.

Bunge is simultaneously managing several large-scale initiatives: an observability and automation journey, a data center consolidation (led by Curtis), a network consolidation (led by Ben Davis), a CMDB/asset management uplift, and ongoing M&A activity — including two new acquisitions adding up to 16 new locations (10 + 6 spin-off).

2. Technical Requirements

2.1 Observability & Automation

  • Data gathering is approximately 75–80% complete; remaining 20–25% requires scheduled follow-up sessions with application teams, end-user computing teams, and cloud teams.
  • Data center and network data gathering is complete.
  • Requirement for a consolidated, standardized observability toolset — currently there is significant fragmentation: multiple tools, different contracts for the same tool type, varying by region and even by country.
  • Tools are being used inconsistently — some only for logging, some only for dashboarding; only a small subset generate actionable alerts tied to the service-and-operations platform.
  • A dedicated ServiceNow/service-operations-aligned team is required to work alongside the observability function — the tool alone is insufficient without the right process and people model.
  • The ServiceNow team is currently evaluating a brownfield vs. greenfield approach for merging four separate instances — this decision directly gates the observability roadmap.
  • Existing tools in scope for integration or replacement decisions include: Microsoft Defender for Cloud (ESCON), VMware/Broadcom upper-layer monitoring, and Oracle native capabilities (already licensed).

2.2 CMDB & Asset Management

  • CMDB is central to all parallel workstreams — observability, data center consolidation, and network consolidation all depend on it.
  • Planned approach: use ServiceNow + LeanIX (both already licensed and integrated) as the CMDB foundation. LeanIX connectivity is already in place — fact sheet updates replicate into the ServiceNow CI database.
  • Current data quality is poor: no single source of truth, no connection to SAP for asset depreciation data, no warranty tracking, and contracts are misclassified (e.g., Oracle DB licensing, Java licensing, and hardware contracts all grouped under one category).
  • Open architectural question: centralize in ServiceNow + LeanIX, or introduce a specialized third-party asset management tool (e.g., Flexera) that feeds both. No decision made.
  • Any selected asset management tool must support bi-directional integration with ServiceNow.
  • Finance/SAP integration is a future requirement: asset depreciation data needs to flow from SAP into the asset management layer.
  • Key dependency: Robert Van Seiko (architecture lead) is on vacation — decision is paused until his return.

2.3 Contract Lifecycle Management (CLM) & Software Asset Management (SAM)

  • Existing procurement tool has a centralized contract repository, but data quality is unreliable — missing data, wrong classifications, duplicate contracts across the same vendor.
  • Manual remediation has already consumed 25+ hours of Tiago's time reclassifying contracts.
  • Requirement: an evergreen, automated SAM/CLM capability (Flexera identified as a candidate) to clean, classify, and maintain contracts — with value tied directly to contract negotiation leverage.
  • Procurement team is running a parallel CLM review across non-technology towers — Flexera may have applicability beyond IT.
  • Tiago used internal Tableau tooling to build a manual asset database — functional but not sustainable as an ongoing solution.

2.4 Data Center Consolidation (Curtis's Workstream)

  • Tiago has limited direct visibility into Curtis's data center consolidation workstream — updates are shared at the weekly Trevor (CTO/boss) meeting level only.
  • CMDB/asset data — specifically asset depreciation and lifecycle data — is a shared dependency between Tiago's observability journey and Curtis's consolidation effort.
  • IBM recommendation: highlight the observability capability to Curtis as a starting point, then show how it enables the consolidation effort (depreciation, lifecycle, discovery).

2.5 M&A & Growth

  • Two new acquisition/divestiture activities in progress since the last IBM conversation (approximately 6–8 weeks ago).
  • Combined scope: ~16 new locations (10 from one acquisition, 6 spin-off locations from the other).
  • These are described as smaller than the Viterra–Bunge integration but are still regionally significant and resource-intensive.
  • M&A activity is creating "moving target" conditions that are delaying roadmap decisions.

3. SWOT Analysis

✔ Strengths
  • ServiceNow and LeanIX are already licensed and integrated — foundation exists
  • LeanIX-to-ServiceNow CI sync is already operational
  • Data center and network data gathering is complete
  • Strong internal awareness of the problem — Tiago has clear visibility of gaps and is actively driving resolution
  • Existing monitoring tools (ESCON, VMware/Broadcom, Oracle) are already licensed, reducing net-new cost
  • Internal Tableau database provides a baseline, even if manual
 ▲ Opportunities
  • Flexera could deliver rapid value for contract cleanup and SAM — low-hanging fruit with immediate ROI in contract negotiations
  • Consolidation of fragmented, duplicate tooling contracts across regions represents significant cost reduction opportunity
  • Procurement team's parallel CLM review could expand Flexera's scope and ROI beyond IT
  • IBM observability capability is a strong fit for Curtis's data center consolidation — depreciation and lifecycle data is a shared win
  • M&A activity (16 new locations) increases urgency and business case for a scalable, evergreen asset management platform
  • Wednesday call with Richard Broca (PMO/influencer) is an opportunity to shape the insource vs. outsource decision
⚠ Weaknesses
  • Poor data quality across CMDB, contracts, and asset records — tools cannot deliver insight on bad data
  • No single source of truth for assets, contracts, depreciation, or warranties
  • No SAP integration for financial/asset data
  • Observability tools used inconsistently — logging only, dashboarding only, few generating real alerts
  • ServiceNow instance consolidation (4 → 1) is unresolved and gates the observability roadmap
  • Key architecture decision-maker (Robert Van Seiko) is currently on vacation
  • Resource constraint: Tiago and team are split across run-the-business, M&A, and transformation simultaneously
 ⛔ Threats / Risks
  • Q1 2027 start date is not confirmed — ServiceNow consolidation dependency could push timelines further right
  • Risk of double work if observability journey starts before ServiceNow consolidation approach is decided
  • Ongoing M&A activity creates moving targets — every new acquisition resets scope and data
  • Without dedicated data stewardship resources, tooling investments will continue to degrade in data quality
  • Red Hat/Lightwell positioning risk (from prior Concert discussion) — fragmented IBM messaging could undermine confidence
  • Insource vs. outsource debate (driven by Richard Broca) could delay or redirect IBM engagement
  • Contract misclassification risk — Oracle licensing example shows exposure to incorrect spend categorization
