## Technical Considerations

### Bungie's Business Model
- **Core Operations:**
  - Commodity procurement from farmers
  - Transportation logistics (rail, truck, barge)
  - Processing operations (milling, crushing)
  - Direct sales and plant processing
  - Oil and grain production

- **Key Insight:** "We're not creating content" - generative AI has limited direct application to physical manufacturing

### Current Organizational Structure (In Flux)
- **Previous:** Separate Business Intelligence/Analytics and Architecture teams
- **New:** Combined BI/Analytics + Architecture organization
- **Concern:** Historical precedent suggests this combination is challenging
- **Impact:** Unclear reporting lines and decision-making authority

### AI Implementation Challenges
1. **FOMO-Driven Approach**
   - Executives pursuing AI because competitors are
   - Lack of clear business case or ROI
   - Cultural shift without strategic foundation

2. **Manufacturing Context**
   - Physical operations don't benefit from content generation
   - Need focus on process optimization, not content creation
   - Logistics, procurement, and back-office are better targets

3. **Data Foundation Requirements**
   - Data governance and architecture must precede AI
   - Records and information management critical
   - Need for data quality and accessibility

### Potential Solution Areas

1. **Procurement Optimization**
   - Commodity pricing and timing decisions
   - Supplier relationship management
   - Contract optimization

2. **Logistics & Supply Chain**
   - Transportation mode optimization
   - Route planning and scheduling
   - Inventory management

3. **Back-Office Automation**
   - HR process improvements
   - IT service management
   - Financial operations

4. **Data Governance**
   - Master data management
   - Data quality frameworks
   - Privacy and compliance (Bob's expertise)
