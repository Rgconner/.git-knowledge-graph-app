Part 1 — Understanding the Threat: What is Mythos?

Overview

Mythos is a frontier AI model developed by Anthropic. Like other advanced LLMs it is highly capable at coding, reasoning, and software engineering — but what sets it apart is its exceptional and unprecedented offensive cybersecurity capability.

Key Facts About Mythos:
  • Has discovered thousands of previously unknown zero-day vulnerabilities across major software platforms and operating systems — flaws no one knew existed, with no patches or mitigations yet available.
  • One confirmed example: a 27-year-old vulnerability that had existed undetected until Mythos surfaced it.
  • 99% of the vulnerabilities Mythos has uncovered are currently unpatched.
  • Can reason across an entire software environment — applications, APIs, and infrastructure — and chain multiple lower-risk weaknesses together into a complete weaponization path, potentially resulting in full system compromise.
  • Operates continuously, at machine speed and at scale — effectively the most capable hacker the world has ever seen, and it is not human.

Project Glasswing — IBM's Early Access

Because of Mythos's danger, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing in April 2026 — a tightly controlled initiative giving select major tech firms, financial institutions, and critical infrastructure operators restricted early access to give the "good guys" a head start. IBM officially joined in May 2026, but had already been receiving exclusive Mythos access from Anthropic well before the public announcement.

US Government Action (Mid-June 2026): The US government temporarily restricted the Mythos V rollout on national security grounds because Anthropic couldn't easily distinguish US vs. foreign users. The restriction lasted only a couple of days. The most capable Mythos model remains available only to approved US organizations.

Why Are Clients Unprepared?

Historically, discovering zero-days and chaining exploit paths required elite human attackers spending significant time. Mythos proved AI can do this automatically and at extraordinary scale. The shared sentiment across 200+ IBM client conversations (end of April through early July 2026): they are completely unprepared.

The old model — only fix "critical" and "high" CVEs, on a weeks-to-months cycle — is now obsolete. Boards and senior leadership are mandating SLA changes from weeks/months to hours or within a day. No organization has the operational model to handle this volume manually.

Part 2 — IBM Concert: The Solution

What is IBM Concert?
IBM Concert is IBM's comprehensive, AI-powered vulnerability management platform that provides end-to-end lifecycle management of vulnerabilities across the entire IT estate — from developer IDE to production systems. In market for a couple of years, it has seen an overnight surge in client interest and board-level urgency driven directly by the Mythos threat.

The Three Things Concert Does

1. Unified Visibility — See Everything

Concert aggregates data from multiple scanners and tools — including Mythos output directly — into a single, unified view of exposure across the entire IT estate. It eliminates the siloed, disjointed scanner data that most enterprises struggle with today.

2. AI-Powered Triage & Prioritization — Focus on What Matters

  • In internal testing, Concert found that only 10% of what Mythos discovered (out of hundreds of thousands of vulnerabilities) needed to be actioned — the rest were false positives or unexploitable in the client's environment.
  • Concert performs deduplication — e.g., reducing 62,000 findings down to a few hundred unique vulnerabilities.
  • Uses a proprietary IBM Research risk score (not standard open-source criticality scores) based on a personalized understanding of the client's specific environment.
  • Maps the blast radius and attack path — even for low-priority vulnerabilities — telling clients whether a vulnerability is actually exploitable in their environment. This directly counters Mythos's chain-exploitation capability.
  • New capability releasing 14 July 2026: powered by IBM Research assets for even deeper attack path analysis.
Cost Example: One client assessed using Claude directly to fix 400,000 vulnerabilities. Projected cost: $35M/month. Concert's triage approach focuses AI only where needed, reducing that cost dramatically.

Speed Benchmark: A human team spent one full week triaging 400,000 Mythos-identified vulnerabilities on an IBM product. Concert performed the same triage in under two minutes.

3. Automated Remediation — Fix It, Fast

  • Shift Left — Secure Coder: A plug-in to developer IDEs (VS Code, Cursor, IBM Bob) that provides real-time vulnerability detection and secure code rewriting recommendations — preventing vulnerabilities from reaching production in the first place.
  • Shift Right — Remediation Agent: Automatically generates full patches for production systems, including pull requests, package upgrades, OS and middleware updates, integration tests, and unit tests — then pushes to production and reports back with continuous post-patch performance tracking.
  • Concert can confidently automate ~90% of a client's vulnerabilities across their entire SDLC.
  • Concert also recommends upgrade paths that minimize production disruption — e.g., recommending a minor version upgrade that fixes security issues with minimal breaks rather than a disruptive major version jump.
  • Integrations include: Vault, Terraform, Packer, and all major scanner outputs.

Part 3 — Clarification: IBM Concert vs. Lightwell

What is Lightwell? Lightwell is a $5 billion IBM + Red Hat initiative to create a clearinghouse that vets and backports open-source vulnerabilities into hardened, secure images. It allows enterprises to stay on their current software version and still receive fixes for zero-day vulnerabilities — without forcing a disruptive major upgrade. Lightwell competes with offerings like ChainGuard.

Important: Some Red Hat sellers have positioned Lightwell (+ Ansible) as a replacement for Concert. This is incorrect. Concert and Lightwell are complementary, not competitive. Lightwell will actually use Concert as one of its tools when it is fully built.
